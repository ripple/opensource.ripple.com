# Sponsored Fees and Reserves

Sponsored Fees and Reserves allows one account to pay transaction fees and reserve requirements on behalf of another account. The account that pays is called the _sponsor_, and the account that benefits is called the _sponsee_. Sponsees maintain full control over their keys and accounts while sponsors handle transaction submission and cover the associated costs.

Without sponsorship, accounts must self-fund both transaction fees and reserves before they can transact on the XRP Ledger. Alternatively, institutions must donate XRP to accounts with no control over how it's used.

Sponsorship provides a mechanism for entities with established XRP balances to subsidize these costs while maintaining strong accountability: both the sponsor and sponsee must sign transactions to authorize the sponsorship, ensuring mutual consent and preventing abuse.

Sponsored Fees and Reserves enables several important use cases:

- **Token distribution**: Issuers can distribute tokens without requiring recipients to hold XRP first.
- **NFT minting**: Creators can enable users to mint NFTs without upfront XRP costs.
- **Enterprise onboarding**: Businesses can onboard customers seamlessly without blockchain friction.
